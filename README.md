# HR Analytics Dashboard — Power BI

## Overview
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of workforce data for an organisation 
of 1,470 employees, designed to support HR decision-making across promotion 
planning, performance management, retention risk, and workforce composition. 
The goal was to translate raw HR data into actionable insights that an HR 
manager or business partner could use at a glance.

## Key Features & Insights
- **Workforce composition** — headcount split by gender (60% male / 40% female) 
  with daily rate comparison across gender groups
- **Promotion pipeline** — identifies 72 employees (4.9%) flagged as due for 
  promotion, supporting talent review cycles
- **Service year distribution** — bar chart showing employee tenure spread, 
  with the largest cohort at 5 years of service (196 employees)
- **Performance by income group** — line chart revealing that average performance 
  rating decreases slightly as income group increases, from Low to High Income
- **Job level distribution** — majority of workforce concentrated at Levels 1 
  and 2 (543 and 534 respectively), informing succession planning needs
- **Layoff risk** — 117 employees (8%) identified as at-risk, with 1,353 (92%) 
  currently on-service
- **Commute distance segmentation** — donut chart showing 63.95% of employees 
  live nearest to the workplace, with 15.58% living very far

## Tools & Techniques
- Power BI Desktop
- DAX measures (KPI card calculations, percentage metrics)
- Multiple visual types: KPI cards, pie chart, bar chart, line chart, donut chart
- Data relationships and filtering across visuals

## Data Source
IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance dataset (publicly available 
on Kaggle) — contains fictional employee records designed for HR analytics 
practice.

## How to Open
Download the `.pbix` file and open in Power BI Desktop 
(free — download from microsoft.com/power-bi).
